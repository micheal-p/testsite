The lifecycle

Six stages. One record.

Every deal carries through the same six stages — from the first memo to administering the live vehicle. Click between stages, never lose context, and the artifacts you upload live on the stage they belong to.

01

Source

Draft the deal memo. Who's the sponsor, what's the ask, why now. Attach the deal letter (PDF) to file it on the Source stage.

02

Structure

Pick the vehicle wrapper — SPV, Sub-Fund, Rolling, Venture, Co-Invest, Roll-Up. Confirm fees, carry, min check, target raise.

03

Outreach

Match the deal to subscribed investors. Track soft → hard → signed commitments with status badges and live progress to target.

04

Diligence

Open the data room to committed investors. Doc access, Q&A, watermarking. Diligence pack scoped to commit status.

05

Close

Generate the subscription package. KYC/AML, e-sign, wire instructions, allocations locked. The activate button moves you to administer.

06

Administer

Vehicle is funded. Cap table, capital calls, distributions, NAV, quarterly LP updates — all anchored to the same deal record.

02 — Vehicles, not products

Six wrappers, one wizard.

SPV, Sub-Fund, Rolling, Venture, Co-Invest, Roll-Up — these used to be six separate product pages. They're now one dropdown inside the wizard. Pick a wrapper; its template defaults populate next step. Behavior — closes, positions, calls, vintages — is type-aware.

  • Type-aware mechanics — single vs multi-close, fixed vs rolling, vintages where it matters
  • Editable template defaults — fees and carry are a starting point, not a cage
  • Position schema is unified — same columns, type-aware reads
  • Switch vehicles mid-draft; terms re-template automatically
03 — Outreach

Soft, hard, signed.

Every commitment lands on the Outreach tab of the deal as a row — name, amount, date, status. Move investors through soft → hard → signed with a single dropdown. The snapshot card shows live committed-vs-target. The sidebar Investors page aggregates everyone across every deal.

  • Investor roster per deal — name, commitment, date, status badge
  • Status workflow: soft → hard → signed, with activity logged on the deal
  • Live progress bar in the snapshot — "committed $X of $Y target"
  • Sidebar Investors page rolls up every commitment across every deal

FAQ

Your questions, answered.

Still wondering? Email the team.

What happened to Fund Administration, SPVs, Sub-Funds, etc.?
They're now stages and wrapper types inside Deal Room. The 6 product pages you might remember (Fund Administration, Investor Management, SPVs, Sub-Funds, Venture, Rolling, Scout, Roll-Up) collapsed into one product with a vehicle dropdown in Step 2 of the wizard. One workspace beats six landing pages that can't survive on their own.
Walk me through the lifecycle.
Source — draft the memo + attach the deal letter. Structure — pick one of six vehicle wrappers, terms pre-fill. Outreach — invite subscribed investors, track soft → hard → signed. Diligence — open the data room to committed investors with doc gating. Close — generate subscription package, KYC/AML, e-sign, lock allocations. Administer — capital calls, distributions, NAV, quarterly LP updates. Every artifact you upload lives on the stage it belongs to.
How are the six vehicles different?
Each wrapper carries template defaults (fees, carry, min check, doc set) AND type-aware mechanics (single vs multi-close, fixed term vs rolling vintages, fund-fee vs no-fee piggyback). The position schema is unified, but the *interpretation* of `commitment`, `called`, `unfunded` shifts by type. Pick a wrapper in Step 2; defaults populate; override what you need.
Where do investors live? What about Investment Management?
Investment Management is folded into Deal Room as the *investor lens*. Open Portfolio in the sidebar to see every position you've committed to across every deal — KPIs (TVPI, DPI, MOIC), per-position multiples, an AI performance summary. The same deal record GPs see, with the camera flipped.
What does the AI summary actually do?
Reads your live position data and writes an analyst-style note: total committed / called / distributed, TVPI verdict against vintage benchmarks, sector exposure + concentration risk, dispersion between best and weakest positions, and a recommendation (hold, stay disciplined, or pause). It's templated logic over your real numbers — fast, deterministic, not investment advice.
Naira or Dollar?
Both. There's a segmented currency toggle in the workspace header. Storage is USD-canonical; display converts on the fly. Every dollar figure across the deal page, snapshot, portfolio KPIs, AI summary, and CSV exports flips together. FX rate is configurable in one place; default is the recent Naira-Dollar parity.
