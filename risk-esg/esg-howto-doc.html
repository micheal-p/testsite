Section 1

What the ESG Clearance Portal is

The ESG Clearance Portal is a paid environmental-audit service operated on the NEIIA platform on behalf of the Federal Ministry of Environment. A Nigerian company applies for an official ESG clearance certificate by submitting its corporate profile and environmental benchmark metrics, uploading its audit logs, and paying the clearance fee through Paystack.

Once payment is confirmed, the application is pushed to the Ministry of Environment's review dashboard. A Ministry officer reviews the submission and either issues the certificate — which is uploaded to the applicant's account and emailed to them — or requests a revision. The certificate carries a number, an issue date and a one-year expiry.

One sentence: the company pays, the Ministry of Environment is notified, the Ministry issues the certificate to the company's account and email.
Section 2

The two sides & the refined flow

The portal has two roles that share one application record:

  • Applicant (company) — completes the four-step wizard, pays, and tracks the application until the certificate arrives.
  • Ministry of Environment (reviewer) — is notified the moment payment lands, reviews the submission, and issues the certificate or requests a revision.
END-TO-END FLOW
  1. The company completes the application and pays the clearance fee (Paystack).
  2. On successful payment, the application is pushed to the Ministry of Environment dashboard, which is auto-notified.
  3. A Ministry officer reviews the company profile, metrics and uploaded audit logs.
  4. The officer issues the certificate — it is uploaded to the applicant's account and a copy is emailed to them — or requests a revision.
  5. The applicant sees the certificate on their dashboard and can view, download or print it.

The sections that follow walk through each side in order, with a screenshot of every screen.

Section 3 · Applicant

Step 1 — Company profile

After signing in, the company clicks Apply for ESG Clearance to open a four-step wizard. The first step shows the company profile, drawn from the registered account details. These fields are read-only here, so the applicant simply confirms they are correct:

  • Company Legal Name (as on CAC)
  • Corporate RC Number
  • Corporate Contact Email
  • Corporate Contact Phone Number
  • Business Sector
Section 4 · Applicant

Step 2 — ESG benchmark metrics

On the second step the company enters its four environmental benchmark figures for the reporting period. These are the numbers the Ministry assesses against national thresholds, so they are entered carefully. Each metric is explained in Section 13.

  • Carbon Emission Intensity (gCO2e / kWh)
  • Energy Efficiency Index (%)
  • Grid Deficit Drawdown (MW)
  • Waste Disposal Rate Classification
Where a company cannot self-report, the step offers an on-site audit path, which flags the metrics as pending a physical Ministry inspection rather than self-declared figures.
Section 5 · Applicant

Step 3 — Upload the audit package

On the third step the company uploads its audit package — the environmental logs or report that support the metrics — as a single PDF. The upload shows a progress bar and, once complete, confirms the file name and size. Uploading a new file replaces the previous one before submission.

Section 6 · Applicant

Step 4 — Pay the clearance fee

The final step shows the tiered clearance fee and a summary of the application. The company clicks Pay & Submit and completes payment through the Paystack gateway. When the payment succeeds, a Paystack reference (for example PSTK_55120934) is recorded against the application and the package is submitted to the Ministry in one action.

Section 7 · Applicant

Tracking your application

After submitting, the company's workspace switches to a live dashboard. It shows the submitted audit package, a status badge (Under Review), and an audit-progress pipeline: profile registered, fee paid, document uploaded, Ministry of Environment review, and clearance stamped. Any decision by the Ministry updates this dashboard automatically.

Section 8 · Ministry of Environment

Notification & the review queue

The moment a company's payment is confirmed, its application appears in the Ministry of Environment's Clearance Console. A green notification banner surfaces the new paid application, the Awaiting Review counter increments, and the application is added to the Incoming Paid Applications table — marked auto-notified on payment.

This is the core of the refined idea: payment is the event that notifies the Ministry. No manual hand-off is needed — paying for the service places the application directly on the Ministry's dashboard.
Section 9 · Ministry of Environment

Reviewing an application

The officer clicks a row to open the review screen. The left panel shows the submitted document with the company's verified Paystack ID, corporate details, and the four environmental performance indicators — each automatically tagged (Acceptable, Excellent, Low Risk, Moderate, High Risk) to guide the assessment. The right panel is the Ministry Assessment, with a remarks field and two decisions.

The two decisions are Approve & Issue Certificate and Request Revision. Requesting a revision sends the officer's remarks back to the applicant's dashboard; the applicant corrects and re-submits without paying again.

Section 10 · Ministry of Environment

Issuing the certificate

When the officer clicks Approve & Issue Certificate, the system generates the official certificate with a unique number (prefix FME-ESG-2026-), an issue date and a one-year expiry. The certificate is then uploaded to the applicant's account and a copy is emailed to the applicant automatically. The Ministry's Certificates Issued count increments.

Section 11 · Applicant

Receiving the certificate (account + email)

On the applicant's side the dashboard updates to Approved. A success banner confirms: "The Federal Ministry of Environment has issued your Audited ESG Clearance Certificate, uploaded it to your account below, and dispatched a copy to your corporate email." The applicant can view, download or print the certificate directly.

Section 12

Handling a revision request

If the Ministry needs changes, the application status becomes Revision and a banner on the applicant's dashboard explains the reason in the officer's own remarks. The applicant reopens the application, corrects the metrics or re-uploads a corrected audit package, and submits again.

Re-submission after a revision does not require a second payment — the original clearance fee covers the cycle. Once corrected, the application returns to the Ministry queue and the review repeats.

Section 15 · Engineers

Engineering notes (data model & wiring)

The current portal is a single page (risk-esg/esg-portal.html) that simulates both roles and the backend in the browser using localStorage. This section maps the demo to what a production build needs.

Roles

The page renders one of two role views, chosen by localStorage.esg_user_role ('client' or 'auditor'). In production these become two authenticated audiences: the company account and the Ministry officer account.

Application record (the shared object)

Every application is one object in the ecn_portal_submissions list. The key fields:

{
  companyName, rcNumber, businessSector,
  corporateEmail, corporatePhone,
  paystackRef, amountPaid,
  manualMetrics: { carbon, efficiency, grid, waste },
  requiresOnSiteAudit,
  submittedFile, fileSize, dateSubmitted,
  status: 'draft' | 'submitted' | 'revision' | 'approved',
  auditorRemarks,
  certificateNum, issueDate, expiryDate
}

The flow in code

  • triggerPaymentSuccess() → submitAuditPackage() sets status:'submitted' and pushes the record to the shared list — this is the payment-notifies-Ministry event.
  • renderAuditorTable() shows the queue and the new-paid-application banner based on the count of submitted records.
  • handleAuditorApproval() sets status:'approved', generates certificateNum / issueDate / expiryDate, and pushes the certificate back onto the applicant record.
  • handleAuditorRevision() sets status:'revision' with the officer's remarks.

Section 16

FAQ

Does the Ministry see the application before payment?

No. The application only reaches the Ministry's dashboard once payment is confirmed. Paying is what notifies the Ministry.

Who issues the certificate?

The Ministry of Environment officer, from the review screen. On approval the certificate is uploaded to the applicant's account and emailed to them automatically.

Does a revision cost another payment?

No. The original clearance fee covers the review cycle, including re-submission after a revision request.

Where does the applicant get the certificate?

Two places: on their portal dashboard (view / download / print) and as a copy emailed to their corporate address.

What is the certificate's validity?

One year from the issue date. The expiry date is printed on the certificate; the company renews before it lapses.

Section 17

Glossary

Applicant — the Nigerian company applying for ESG clearance.

Ministry of Environment — the reviewing authority that is notified on payment and issues the certificate.

Clearance Console — the Ministry's dashboard: queue, review screen and issued certificates.

Audit package — the environmental logs/report the applicant uploads to support its metrics.

Paystack reference — the unique ID of the clearance payment; proof that the fee was paid.

ESG benchmark metrics — the four environmental indicators the Ministry assesses.

On-site audit — the path for companies whose metrics require a physical Ministry inspection.

Certificate number — the unique ESG clearance ID (prefix FME-ESG-2026-).

NEIIA — the platform operator hosting the portal for the Ministry.
