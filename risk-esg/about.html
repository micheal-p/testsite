Understanding the Mandatory
ESG Clearance Requirement.

Environmental, Social, and Governance (ESG) compliance is no longer optional for businesses operating within Nigeria's critical energy, manufacturing, and industrial sectors. Mandated by the Federal Ministry of Environment, the ESG Clearance Certificate serves as the ultimate proof of a company's commitment to sustainable practices.

Why is ESG Clearance Compulsory?

Under the latest National Environmental Standards and Regulations directives, all expanding local businesses, SMEs, and large-scale industries must undergo periodic assessments. This ensures that industrial growth does not come at the cost of environmental degradation, public health, or ethical governance.

Without a valid ESG Clearance Certificate, companies face regulatory bottlenecks, including:

  • Inability to secure government contracts or grants.
  • Suspension of operational licenses for high-risk sites.
  • Denial of access to green financing and international investment funds.

What the Audit Entails

The ESG Clearance Portal streamlines the verification process. Depending on your operational scale, you will be required to undergo either a Baseline Gap Analysis or a Full Third-Party Certification. Evaluators from NEIIA and the Ministry of Environment will verify:

Environmental Impact

Carbon emission intensity, waste water treatment protocols, and overall ecological footprint.

Social & Governance

Corporate governance charters, labor practices, and community impact assessments.

The Clearance Pipeline

Our portal connects your submitted documentation directly to NEIIA auditors. Once your files and payment are securely validated, your application enters the fast-tracked review queue. Successful applicants receive a digitally stamped Clearance Certificate directly to their dashboard.
