Access

Open data for national development, free of cost

The National Energy Data Bank is a public good. NEDB provides open access to Nigeria's official energy statistics — no paywalls, no registration required for basic data access.

Data Bank includes:

  • Full petroleum statistics — crude oil, PMS, LPG, AGO, HHK, DPK, ATK, FO
  • Electricity generation, consumption & distribution data
  • Biomass & coal statistics (fuelwood, charcoal, coal export)
  • Energy balance tables — gas and national energy balances
  • EnerGIS interactive geographic information system
  • Socio-economic indicators — GDP & energy prices

For institutional data partnerships or API access, contact us at databank@energy.gov.ng

FAQs

Your questions answered

The NEDB is Nigeria's official energy statistics platform managed by the Energy Commission of Nigeria (ECN). It provides verified data on petroleum products, electricity, biomass, coal, natural gas, and socio-economic indicators to support evidence-based energy planning.

Yes. NEDB is a public resource. All energy statistics, charts, and downloadable reports are freely accessible. The platform is funded by the Energy Commission of Nigeria as part of its mandate to promote energy data transparency.

Key indicators like crude oil prices, pump prices, and electricity supply are updated regularly. Annual statistics are published after official verification from data-producing agencies. Historical data goes back to the 1960s for some datasets.

EnerGIS is the Nigerian Energy Geographic Information System — an interactive map that lets users visualise energy infrastructure, oil and gas fields, power generation stations, and distribution networks spatially across Nigeria.

Yes. NEDB supports report generation and data export in PDF and Excel formats. You can filter by sector, year range, and product type before generating a custom report.

Data is sourced from ECN, NNPC Limited, NERC, CBN, NBS, and NUPRC (formerly DPR).
